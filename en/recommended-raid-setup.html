Best NAS & HDD for Each RAID Setup

Even if you understand RAID, choosing the right NAS and HDD can be confusing. This guide introduces recommended configurations based on your needs.

Beginner (2-Bay NAS · RAID 1)

For home use and data safety, RAID 1 is the most popular choice.

  • Recommended NAS: Synology DS223
  • Recommended HDD: IronWolf Pro 4TB ×2

This setup is ideal for beginners:

  • Easy to set up and manage
  • Mirroring protects your data from drive failure

👉 Check Synology DS223 on Amazon

👉 Check IronWolf Pro 4TB on Amazon

Note: NAS devices do NOT include HDDs. Make sure to purchase drives separately.

Capacity-Oriented (4-Bay NAS · RAID 5)

RAID 5 offers a good balance between storage capacity and redundancy.

  • Recommended NAS: Synology DS423+
  • Recommended HDD: IronWolf 8TB ×4

RAID 5 is one of the most popular configurations for home NAS users.

👉 Check Synology DS423+ on Amazon

👉 Check IronWolf series on Amazon

Note: Availability may vary. Please check current stock status.

High Reliability (RAID 6)

Best for important data and long-term storage.

  • Recommended HDD: IronWolf Pro Series

RAID 6 can tolerate up to two disk failures, making it ideal for critical data.

👉 Check IronWolf Pro on Amazon

Performance-Oriented (RAID 10)

Suitable for high-speed workloads.

  • Use cases: Virtual machines, video editing

RAID 10 provides excellent performance but reduces usable capacity.

Quick Recommendation

  • 2 drives → RAID 1
  • 4+ drives → RAID 5
  • Critical data → RAID 6
