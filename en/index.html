Quick RAID Overview

  • RAID 0: No redundancy, maximum capacity
  • RAID 1: Mirroring, lower capacity efficiency
  • RAID 5: Uses 1 drive worth of parity
  • RAID 6: Uses 2 drives worth of parity
  • RAID 10: Mirror + striping, requires even number of drives

Which RAID Should You Choose?

  • Maximize capacity: RAID 0
  • Data safety: RAID 1 / RAID 6
  • Balanced choice: RAID 5
  • Performance + redundancy: RAID 10

How RAID Capacity Is Calculated

Usable capacity depends on the RAID level:

  • RAID 0: Total of all drives
  • RAID 1: Capacity of one drive
  • RAID 5: (Number of drives - 1) × size
  • RAID 6: (Number of drives - 2) × size
  • RAID 10: (Number of drives / 2) × size

Example: 4 drives × 8TB in RAID 5 → (4 - 1) × 8 = 24TB

RAID Is Not a Backup

RAID protects against drive failure, but not against accidental deletion, malware, ransomware, or disasters. Always keep separate backups of important data.
