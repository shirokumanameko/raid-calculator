Recommended RAID Setup (NAS & HDD Guide)

Choosing a RAID level is only half the decision. You also need the right NAS and hard drives. This guide shows simple, practical configurations for beginners to advanced users.

Beginner Setup (2-Bay NAS / RAID 1)

Best for home users who want safe and simple storage.

  • Recommended NAS: Synology DS223
  • Recommended HDD: Seagate IronWolf 4TB ×2

Easy to set up and reliable for storing photos, videos, and backups.

Balanced Setup (4-Bay NAS / RAID 5)

Good balance of storage capacity and data protection.

  • Recommended NAS: Synology DS423+
  • Recommended HDD: Seagate IronWolf 8TB ×4

RAID 5 is one of the most popular configurations for home NAS users.

👉 View this setup on Amazon

High Safety Setup (RAID 6)

Recommended for important or long-term data storage.

  • Recommended HDD: Seagate IronWolf Pro

Can survive up to two disk failures, making it ideal for business or critical data.

👉 View compatible HDDs on Amazon

High Performance Setup (RAID 10)

Best for speed-intensive workloads.

  • Use cases: Virtual machines, video editing

Offers excellent performance but lower storage efficiency.

If You're Not Sure

  • 2 drives → RAID 1
  • 4+ drives → RAID 5
  • Critical data → RAID 6
