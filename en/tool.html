Quick RAID Overview

  • RAID 0: No redundancy, maximum capacity
  • RAID 1: Mirroring, lower usable capacity
  • RAID 5: Uses one disk for parity
  • RAID 6: Uses two disks for parity
  • RAID 10: Combination of mirroring and striping (requires even number of disks)

Which RAID Should You Choose?

  • Maximize capacity: RAID 0
  • High data safety: RAID 1 / RAID 6
  • Balanced option: RAID 5
  • Performance + safety: RAID 10

How RAID Capacity Is Calculated

Usable capacity depends on the RAID level:

  • RAID 0: Total of all disks
  • RAID 1: Capacity of one disk
  • RAID 5: (Number of disks - 1) × disk size
  • RAID 6: (Number of disks - 2) × disk size
  • RAID 10: (Number of disks / 2) × disk size

Example: 4 disks × 8TB in RAID 5 → (4 - 1) × 8 = 24TB

RAID Is Not a Backup

RAID improves fault tolerance against disk failures, but it does not protect against accidental deletion, malware, ransomware, or disasters. Always keep a separate backup of important data.
